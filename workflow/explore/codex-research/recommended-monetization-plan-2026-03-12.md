# Recommended Monetization Plan

**Date:** 2026-03-12
**Goal:** Make money from Obey Agent Economy without pretending every subsystem is already commercially proven

## Bottom Line

Do not try to monetize the whole system at once.

Monetize the parts that are already credible:

1. **grants and ecosystem funding**
2. **fixed-scope services around risk and reliability**
3. **design-partner work using the strongest modules**

Treat the rest, especially the still-not-fully-proven `0G` inference path, as leverage for grants and differentiation rather than the main sale.

## The Best Current Wedge

The best wedge is:

**"auditable agent execution and runtime risk controls for teams building onchain automation."**

Why this wedge works:

- it does not require a full autonomous economy to be live
- it uses the strongest parts of the repo
- it maps to pain that teams already understand
- it can be sold as a service before it is sold as a product

The best modules for that wedge are:

- `cre-risk-router`
- `agent-coordinator`
- `dashboard`
- selected patterns from `agent-defi`

`agent-inference` and `0G` should support the story, not carry it.

## Priority Order

## 1. Fastest money: grants and retro funding

### Base

Base is a strong immediate path because:

- the grants are retroactive
- the weekly rewards are small but real
- you do not need the whole cross-chain system to be proven
- the Base-specific integrations can be shown independently

Recommended pitch:

- accountable onchain agent execution on Base
- ERC-8004 identity
- ERC-8021 attribution
- x402 machine payments

Do not oversell live PnL. Sell the implementation quality and shipped integrations.

### 0G

0G is a good grant target, but the pitch should be:

- "we have real integration and chain evidence"
- "the final proof gap is authenticated inference execution"
- "grant funding helps us turn a rare multi-service testnet integration into a public mainnet-ready reference"

That is more credible than claiming the full pipeline is already complete.

### Hedera and Hashgraph

These are slower but still worthwhile because:

- the coordinator and HCS story are real
- `hiero-plugin` is a clean ecosystem-enablement angle
- "developer tooling + agent coordination patterns" is easier to sell than "full autonomous agent economy"

### Chainlink

Do not lead with BUILD unless you want a token-dependent partnership motion.

Lead with:

- CRE-based risk controls
- reference implementation value
- Community Grants if you want a grant-shaped path

## 2. Highest-probability revenue: service packages

You should package 2-3 offers and try to sell them directly.

### Offer A: Risk Controls Integration Sprint

Who it is for:

- teams shipping bots, agents, or onchain automation

What you sell:

- pre-execution risk gates
- deny/approve policy layer
- evidence logging
- deployment and runbook

Why it works:

- narrow scope
- easy to explain
- does not depend on 0G

### Offer B: Agent Reliability Audit

Who it is for:

- teams with agent demos that break under real conditions

What you sell:

- architecture review
- failure-mode analysis
- observability gaps
- implementation recommendations

Why it works:

- very low customer commitment
- easiest first paid engagement
- converts cleanly into follow-on implementation work

### Offer C: Design-Partner Pilot

Who it is for:

- protocols or infra teams that want a credible agent demo quickly

What you sell:

- a 2-4 week pilot using your existing components
- one chain or one workflow only
- hard scope and hard deadline

Why it works:

- you already have the components
- buyers pay for speed and credibility, not novelty alone

## 3. What not to lead with

Do not lead with:

- a broad orchestration platform sale
- a standalone Risk Router API business
- "autonomous hedge fund" messaging
- a token launch
- a 0G-first commercialization story

These are either too early, too distribution-heavy, or too dependent on proof you do not yet have.

## What To Say Publicly

The strongest public message is:

> Obey Agent Economy helps teams move from fragile agent demos to auditable, risk-controlled onchain automation.

That statement is easier to defend than:

> We built a fully autonomous multi-chain agent economy ready for production.

The first sells.
The second invites skepticism.

## 14-Day Execution Plan

## Days 1-3: tighten the proof

1. Build one clean public evidence page with links to the strongest on-chain proof you already have
2. Record one short demo showing the risk-router and coordinator flow
3. Rewrite the 0G and Base grant language to remove overclaims

## Days 4-6: package the offers

1. Write a one-pager for:
   - Risk Controls Integration Sprint
   - Agent Reliability Audit
   - Design-Partner Pilot
2. Put price bands on them
3. Define exact deliverables and exclusions

## Days 7-10: apply for money already on the table

1. Base Builder Grants
2. Base Builder Rewards
3. 0G Guild
4. Hedera Foundation
5. Chainlink Community Grants

## Days 11-14: test for paid demand

1. Reach out to 20-40 target teams
2. Offer the audit first, not the large build
3. Aim for one paid discovery or one fixed-scope sprint

## Decision Rule

If you want the shortest honest version:

- **Use grants for immediate non-dilutive funding**
- **Use services for first real customer revenue**
- **Use 0G as a differentiator, not the main dependency**

If one thing has to happen next, it is this:

**sell one narrow paid engagement before building any more platform surface area.**
