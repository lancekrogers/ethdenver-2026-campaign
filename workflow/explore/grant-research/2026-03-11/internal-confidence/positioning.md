# Internal Positioning

This is the stronger private narrative for Obey Agent Economy. Use it to guide strategy, not as copy-paste public wording.

## Internal Belief

Obey Agent Economy is not just a hackathon artifact. It is the early version of a serious agent-infrastructure business with multiple monetization paths:

- ecosystem grants
- agent risk and reliability services
- design-partner pilots
- later productization around auditable agent execution

## Strong Internal Framing

- The repo is unusually broad and technically differentiated for a solo build
- `cre-risk-router` plus `agent-coordinator` is already enough to sell a narrow service offer
- Base gives the cleanest short-term retroactive funding story
- 0G gives the strongest upside if the final inference proof gap is closed
- Hedera and Hashgraph strengthen the "serious infrastructure" story

## What We Can Believe Internally But Should Phrase Carefully Externally

### 1. "This could become a real business"

Internally: yes

Externally:

- "service-led commercialization path"
- "design-partner opportunity"
- "grant-backed path to mainnet readiness"

### 2. "The Base agent can become self-sustaining"

Internally: yes, that is the design thesis

Externally:

- "implemented testnet economics"
- "cost-aware runtime with P&L tracking"
- "more live trading evidence in progress"

### 3. "The 0G stack is strategically important"

Internally: yes, because it is rare and differentiating

Externally:

- "multi-service 0G integration with real deployment evidence"
- "authenticated inference execution remains the final public proof gap"

### 4. "The whole system is more than a demo"

Internally: yes

Externally:

- "working multi-agent system"
- "core components have deployed contracts and real transactions"
- "remaining proof gaps are explicitly tracked"

## Practical Rule

Use the internal-confidence track to decide what to pursue.
Use the external-proof track to decide what to claim.
