## 1. Base Builder Grants (Retroactive)

**Odds: 50–65%** — Your best shot at fast money.

### Why Relatively High
- **Retroactive = shipped code wins.** No pitch deck, no roadmap review. You've already built it.
- **Small amounts (1–5 ETH / ~$2K–$10K).** Low bar for the grantor to say yes.
- **Discovery-based.** Their team scouts Twitter/Farcaster. You just need to be visible.
- **You integrate 3 Base-native standards** (ERC-8004, ERC-8021, x402) which are new and under-adopted. Base has incentive to showcase early adopters.

### What Could Kill It
- Your repos may not be public yet — Base team can't scout what they can't see.
- You don't appear to have a Farcaster presence or Base-focused social posts. Their discovery mechanism is social-first.
- Self-nomination link goes to a Paragraph post, not a structured application. It's informal and opaque.

### How to Improve Odds
1. **Make agent-defi repo public immediately.** Non-negotiable.
2. **Post a thread on Farcaster/Twitter** showing ERC-8004 + ERC-8021 + x402 working together. Tag @base, @jessepollak, @cdixon. Include tx links.
3. **Self-nominate** via [the form](https://paragraph.com/@grants.base.eth/calling-based-builders). Keep it short — link to repo, link to tx, one paragraph.
4. **Register on [builderscore.xyz](https://www.builderscore.xyz/)** for weekly 2 ETH rewards. Separate from grants.
5. **Timeline to money:** 2–6 weeks if they notice you. Could be faster with social visibility.

## 2. 0G Guild / Ecosystem Growth Program

**Odds: 35–50%** — Strong fit, but early-stage program with unclear selection criteria.

### Why Decent
- **You use all 4 0G services.** Most applicants use 1-2. This is genuinely rare.
- **$88.88M fund announced Feb 2025.** Fund is fresh and needs to deploy capital. Early applicants get priority (their words).
- **300+ partnered projects** already in ecosystem — they're actively growing.
- **Rolling applications, 2-4 week review, then qualification call.** Structured but not slow.
- **They spotlight Guild projects on their blog** (Zer0, H1uman, Avinasi Labs, Leea Labs, Unagi, Rivalz) — they want to show activity.

### What Could Kill It
- **0G is pre-mainnet.** The foundation may prioritize projects that help drive mainnet launch, not just testnet demos.
- **KYC required.** Standard but adds friction.
- **Milestone-based disbursement.** You don't get a lump sum. You get paid per milestone completed. This means you need to actually deliver the roadmap.
- **Your project isn't live on mainnet.** Testnet integration is strong but they may want mainnet-ready projects.
- **Competition:** AI agent projects are the hot category right now. You're competing with well-funded teams.

### How to Improve Odds
1. **Apply via [guild.0gfoundation.ai](https://guild.0gfoundation.ai/)** immediately. Rolling = first-mover advantage.
2. **Frame your ask around mainnet migration.** "We've proven the integration on Galileo. Grant funds testnet→mainnet transition."
3. **Propose concrete milestones** they can verify: mainnet deployment, X transactions, open-source SDK release.
4. **Reference all 4 services by name** in the application. Make the multi-service integration impossible to miss.
5. **Prepare a demo video** before the qualification call. They'll want to see it working.
6. **Timeline to money:** 4–8 weeks from application to first milestone payment.

## 3. Hedera Foundation Proposal Grant

**Odds: 25–40%** — Solid fit but bureaucratic process.

### Why Moderate
- **Deep native integration.** HCS + HTS + HIP-1215 + Account Management. You're using Hedera the way Hedera wants to be used.
- **Developer tooling angle.** hiero-plugin with 3 Hedera templates is directly ecosystem-building.
- **"No Solidity" narrative** aligns with Hedera's differentiation story (native services > EVM).
- **They've deployed billions of HBAR** in ecosystem grants historically. Money exists.

### What Could Kill It
- **Bureaucratic process.** Panel review → due diligence → HBAR Committee → Grant Committee → Legal/KYC → contracting → milestone payments. This is months, not weeks.
- **Hedera ecosystem has consolidated.** HBAR Foundation underwent leadership changes in 2024. New "Hedera Foundation" structure may have different priorities.
- **You're a solo builder.** Grant committees tend to favor teams, not individuals.
- **No mainnet deployment.** Everything is on testnet.
- **Unclear if Hedera Foundation is actively deploying grants** at the same rate as the old HBAR Foundation. The 7B HBAR allocation was announced but operational status is murky.

### How to Improve Odds
1. **Submit via [hedera.foundation/submit-a-proposal](https://hedera.foundation/submit-a-proposal)** with a clear, concise proposal.
2. **Lead with hiero-plugin.** Developer tooling has clearer ecosystem ROI than "agent coordination."
3. **Propose an SDK extraction** — "reusable HCS messaging library for Go agent builders." This benefits other Hedera developers, not just you.
4. **Ask for a small amount first** ($25K–$50K). Lower asks clear committees faster.
5. **Get a warm intro** if possible. Hedera ecosystem is relationship-driven. Attend Hedera community calls, get visible in their Discord.
6. **Timeline to money:** 3–6 months realistically, given the multi-committee approval process.

## 4. Chainlink BUILD Program

**Odds: 15–25%** — Significant hidden barrier I need to flag.

### Critical Issue: Token Commitment Required

**This is not a grant program.** Chainlink BUILD requires participating projects to commit **a percentage of their native token supply** to Chainlink service providers (stakers). From the [Chainlink blog](https://blog.chain.link/chainlink-build-program/):

> "Participating projects will be committing multiple percentage points of their total token supply to the Build program."

**You don't have a token.** This means BUILD as currently structured may not be accessible to you unless:
- You're planning to launch a token
- They've changed the requirement for infrastructure/tooling projects
- There's a separate track for non-token projects

### Why It's Still Worth Exploring
- **90+ projects already in BUILD.** It's a real, active program.
- **CRE is new tech** and you're an early adopter with real feedback. Chainlink may want to showcase CRE builders regardless of token status.
- **Season 1 of Chainlink Rewards** just launched (Nov 2025) featuring BUILD projects. Being in BUILD = marketing amplification.
- **Your CRE developer feedback** is genuinely valuable to them.

### What Could Kill It
- **Token requirement.** Biggest barrier. May be a non-starter without a token.
- **They have 90+ projects already.** Selectivity is increasing.
- **CRE is still early.** They may not be ready to BUILD-ify CRE projects yet.
- **Solo builder.** BUILD skews toward established projects with teams.

### How to Improve Odds
1. **Apply via [chain.link/build-program](https://chain.link/build-program)** but be transparent about not having a token.
2. **Reframe as infrastructure.** "CRE Risk Router is infrastructure other BUILD projects can use. We don't need a token — we need technical access and co-marketing."
3. **Lead with the developer feedback.** You've already written detailed CRE experience feedback. This shows partnership value beyond token commitment.
4. **Alternative: Chainlink Community Grants** (separate from BUILD) may accept non-token projects. Check [go.chain.link/archives/community/grants](https://go.chain.link/archives/community/grants).
5. **Get visible in Chainlink developer community.** SmartCon, CRE Discord, developer forums.
6. **Timeline to money:** BUILD isn't cash grants — it's services, access, and co-marketing. If you want actual money from Chainlink, Community Grants is the path.

- BUILD requires token commitment: Verified via [Chainlink blog](https://blog.chain.link/chainlink-build-program/) ✅
- 90+ projects in BUILD: Verified via [RootData](https://www.rootdata.com/Archives/detail/The%20Projects%20Joined%20in%20the%20Chainlink%20BUILD%20Program?k=MTU0NQ%3D%3D) ✅

- "Long-term support" framing: Verified ✅
